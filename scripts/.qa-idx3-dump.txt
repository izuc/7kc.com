prep=10 cook=8 servings=4
-- INGREDIENTS --
  * beef_steak | 500 g
  * cucumber | 1
  * cherry_tomato | 200 g
  * mint | handful
  * coriander | handful
  * fish_sauce | 2 tbsp
  * lime | 2
  * chilli | 1
  * onion_red | 1/2
-- STEPS --
1. Sear steak rare, rest, slice.
      detail: Pat steak dry, season heavily with salt and pepper. Hot pan, splash of oil. Sear 90 sec a side — rare is traditional, the warm beef cooks slightly in the dressing. 2 min a side for medium-rare if rare isn't your thing. Rest 5 min on a board (this is critical — slicing straight away loses all the juice). Slice very thin ACROSS the grain.

3. Fan beef over the top.
      detail: Fan beef slices over the top of the dressed salad on a big platter — DON'T toss through. The beef wants to sit on top for presentation and so the juices mingle with the dressing. Pour any resting juices from the board over the top. Extra herbs, extra chilli if you love heat, crushed peanuts for crunch.

      detail: Cube chicken thigh into 3cm pieces. In a bowl: yoghurt, 1 tsp turmeric, 2 tbsp curry powder, salt, a squeeze of lemon if you have it.       detail: Knob of butter in a heavy pan over medium-high till foaming.       detail: Chicken back in with any resting juices. Simmer very gently for 10 min till the chicken is cooked through (check by cutting one in half — no pink). Taste for salt and a pinch of sugar if the tomato is sharp. A knob of extra butter at the end rounds it to silk.

      detail: Fluff basmati. Ladle butter chicken into shallow bowls, rice alongside (not under — it goes mushy). Tear a big handful of coriander over the top. Naan if you have it, for mopping the sauce. A dollop of extra yoghurt cools the spice for anyone who wants it.

  * lemon | 1
  * tomato | 1
-- STEPS --

      detail: Grate a red onion on the coarse side of a box grater into a sieve or tea towel. Squeeze HARD to remove the juice — wet onion makes kofta fall apart on the grill. Liquid out, oniony pulp left behind. Counterintuitive but essential.

      detail: Big bowl: 700g lamb mince, the squeezed grated onion, 3 grated garlic cloves, 2 tsp cumin, 1 tsp paprika, salt, pepper, half a handful of finely chopped mint.       detail: Warm flatbreads 15 sec a side on the hot grill. Smear tzatziki down the middle, slide two kofta off the skewer on top. Squeeze of lemon, sliced tomato, extra mint. Roll tight, eat standing up. Mess is expected.

  * butter | 120 g
  * lemon | 1/2
  * dijon | 1 tsp
  * parsley | handful
  * vinegar_balsamic | splash (for poaching)
-- STEPS --
1. Separate 2 eggs; whisk yolks with lemon and dijon.
      detail: Separate two eggs — crack on a flat surface, gently pour the yolk between the two shell halves letting the white fall into a bowl, yolks into a heatproof bowl. Whisk yolks with 1 tsp dijon and a squeeze of lemon juice till pale and slightly thickened, 1 min. The dijon helps stabilise the emulsion.
2. Stream in melted butter, whisking, for hollandaise.
      detail: Melt 120g butter in a small pan till fully liquid and foaming. Set the yolk bowl over a pan of barely-simmering water (don't let it touch the water — heats too fast). Whisking CONSTANTLY, stream in the hot butter in a slow drizzle. Goes from thin to thick in 2 min. If it splits: a splash of hot water and vigorous whisking usually saves it. Salt, pepper. Set over a pot of warm water off the heat to keep loose.
3. Toast split muffins, warm ham briefly.
      detail: Split muffins with a fork (tearing gives more craggy surface than cutting). Toast till deep gold. Warm ham slices in a dry pan 30 sec a side — cold meat ruins a hot dish.
4. Poach remaining eggs in simmering vinegar water, 3 min.
      detail: Saucepan with 10cm of water and a splash of vinegar, bare simmer (NOT boiling). Crack each egg into a ramekin first. Stir the water to create a whirlpool, slide egg into the centre. 3 min for set whites and runny yolk. Lift out with a slotted spoon, rest on a tea towel briefly.
5. Stack muffin, ham, egg, spoon hollandaise.
      detail: Muffin half toast-side-up, slice of warm ham folded on top, poached egg balanced on that. Spoon generous hollandaise over so it cascades down the sides — don't be stingy. That ribbon of yellow is the whole point.
6. Scatter parsley.
      detail: Torn parsley or chives scattered over. Crack of pepper, tiny pinch of paprika for colour. Eat RIGHT AWAY — hollandaise congeals as it cools and poached eggs go sad. Breakfast worth setting an alarm for.

SLUG: pumpkin-soup | TITLE: Pumpkin Soup with Crispy Sage | TAGS: ['vegetarian', 'soup', 'comfort', 'batch-friendly']
prep=10 cook=30 servings=4
-- INGREDIENTS --
  * pumpkin | 1 kg
  * onion_brown | 1
  * garlic | 3 cloves
  * stock_veg | 1 L
  * cream | 150 ml
  * butter | 30 g
  * sage | handful
  * bread | to serve
-- STEPS --
1. Soften onion and garlic in butter.
      detail: Finely chop a brown onion and grate 3 garlic cloves. Melt 30g butter in a heavy pot over medium. Onion with a pinch of salt, 8 min till soft and translucent. Garlic for the last minute — any longer at the bottom of a hot pot and it burns, turning the soup bitter.
2. Add cubed pumpkin, stock, simmer 25 min.
      detail: Cube 1kg pumpkin into 3cm chunks (skin on is fine — softens in the simmer, adds nutrition). Tip into the pot with a litre of vegetable stock. Bring to a simmer, lid ajar. 25 min till the pumpkin falls apart when prodded — don't blitz before it's fully soft or you'll get lumps.
3. Fry sage leaves till crisp.
      detail: While the soup simmers: a small pan with a knob of butter over medium till foaming. Whole sage leaves in, 30 sec a side till vivid green and crisp. Lift onto paper towel with tongs, sprinkle salt. They shatter like chips — garnish gold.
4. Blitz soup with cream till silky.
      detail: Off heat. Pour in 150ml cream. Stick blender straight in the pot (safest) or blender in batches (cover lid with a tea towel — hot liquid creates steam pressure). Blitz 2 min till silky and smooth, no lumps.
5. Season well, top with sage, bread on the side.
      detail: Season well — soup always needs more salt than you think. Crack of pepper, a squeeze of lemon or splash of balsamic lifts it. Ladle into bowls, swirl of extra cream on top, three sage leaves perched, crusty bread on the side for dunking. Winter in a bowl.

prep=10 cook=8 servings=2
-- INGREDIENTS --
  * beef_steak | 400 g
  * broccoli | 1 head
  * snow_pea | 150 g
  * bok_choy | 2 heads
  * garlic | 3 cloves
  * ginger | thumb
  * soy_sauce | 3 tbsp
  * sesame_oil | 1 tbsp
  * noodle_egg | 200 g
-- STEPS --
1. Slice beef thin, toss with 1 tbsp soy.
      detail: Freeze beef 15 min first for easier slicing. Cut ACROSS the grain into 3mm strips. Toss in a bowl with 1 tbsp soy sauce and 1 tsp cornflour if you have it. Cornflour creates a velvet coating that keeps meat juicy. Rest 10 min while you prep everything else.
2. Blanch noodles.
      detail: Big pot of water to a rolling boil. Egg noodles in, cook 60 sec less than packet (they'll finish in the wok). Drain, rinse briefly under cold water to stop sticking, toss with a tiny splash of sesame oil so they don't clump. Set aside.
3. Blast beef in hot wok 90 sec, remove.
      detail: Wok smoking-hot — drop of water should vaporise on contact. 1 tbsp oil, swirl. Beef in a single layer, don't touch for 60 sec — let it sear. Then toss fast for 30 more sec till edges are deep brown, centres still pink. Out to a plate.
4. Stir-fry garlic, ginger, broccoli, snow peas, bok choy.
      detail: Same wok, more oil. 3 grated garlic cloves and a thumb of grated ginger for 30 sec till fragrant. Then broccoli florets, snow peas, halved bok choy — toss hard 2 min. Veg should stay bright and crunchy with a bit of char, not limp.

      detail: Beef back in with resting juices. Splash 2 more tbsp soy and 1 tbsp sesame oil. Toss 30 sec till everything's glossy.
6. Toss through noodles.
      detail: Tip the noodles in, toss another 60 sec till they're coated in sauce and everything's married. Splash of water if it looks dry. Into bowls immediately — this meal doesn't wait.

SLUG: pancakes-fluffy | TITLE: Fluffy Buttermilk Pancakes | TAGS: ['breakfast', 'family']
prep=10 cook=15 servings=4
-- INGREDIENTS --
  * flour | 2 cups
  * baking_powder | 2 tsp
  * sugar | 3 tbsp
  * eggs | 2
  * buttermilk | 2 cups
  * butter | 60 g
  * vanilla | 1 tsp
  * maple_syrup | to serve
  * blueberry | 1 cup
-- STEPS --
1. Whisk flour, baking powder, sugar.
      detail: Big bowl: 2 cups flour, 2 tsp baking powder, 3 tbsp sugar, pinch of salt. Whisk together to break up lumps and distribute the raising agent evenly. The bicarb is activated by the buttermilk acids later — patches of it unmixed will leave bitter spots.
2. Mix eggs, buttermilk, melted butter, vanilla.
      detail: Separate jug: 2 eggs cracked in, 2 cups buttermilk, 60g melted and cooled butter, 1 tsp vanilla. Beat with a fork till just combined — yolks broken up. The butter needs to be cooled or it'll scramble the eggs; pour it in last and quickly.
3. Combine gently — lumps are fine.
      detail: Tip wet into dry and fold with a spatula till JUST combined. Lumps are GOOD. Overmixed batter develops gluten and gives you tough flat pancakes. A streak of flour still visible means you should stop.
4. Rest 5 min.
      detail: Cover with a tea towel, rest 5 min on the bench. The flour hydrates, the gluten relaxes, the baking powder starts its lift. Skipping rest = denser pancakes.
5. Cook 2 min a side in buttered pan.
      detail: Non-stick pan over medium (NOT high — burns the outside before the inside cooks). Swirl a small knob of butter. Ladle 1/4 cup batter per pancake. Wait till bubbles form and pop on the surface, 2 min. Flip once. 90 sec the other side. Don't press — squashes air out.
6. Stack with blueberries and maple.
      detail: Stack five high on a warm plate. Pat of butter melting down between them, maple syrup poured generously, blueberries scattered. Eat fast; no one likes cold pancakes.
