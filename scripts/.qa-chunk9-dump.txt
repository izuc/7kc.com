--- STEPS ---
  (1) Bloom the saffron in a splash of warm water.
        detail: Crush the saffron threads between your fingers into a small cup and cover with 2 tbsp of warm (not boiling) water. Fork through the rice with the pine nuts, preserved lemon and parsley, lifting the grains apart rather than stirring.

        detail: This is the secret to a kale salad. Put the ribbons in a big bowl with a pinch of salt and a teaspoon of the oil, and scrunch and rub it with your hands for a full minute.         detail: Tear or cube the bread into rough 2cm chunks and toss with a drizzle of oil and salt. Spread on a tray and bake at 200C for 8 to 10 min, or toast in a pan, until deeply golden and crunchy through. Rough torn edges catch more dressing than neat cubes.

        detail: In a jar or bowl combine the mayo, dijon, juice of the lemon, a grated clove of garlic and half the parmesan finely grated. Whisk in the olive oil until creamy. A traditional caesar uses anchovy and raw yolk; this mayo version is the easy, reliable route.
  (5) Toss and serve.
        detail: Pour most of the dressing over the massaged kale and toss hard so every ribbon is coated. Add the croutons and toss again.
